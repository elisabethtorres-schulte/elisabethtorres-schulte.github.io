Beyond Exorcism: Novel Understandings of Mental Illness Through Catholic Art

Poster & Abstract

This grant-funded project examined portrayals of saints in Catholic art who exhibited behaviors consistent with modern mental health diagnoses. By situating these portrayals in their socio-cultural context, I argued that the line between pathology and normalcy is socially contingent, heavily shaped by both societal norms and biology.

Supporting Classwork- Between the Lines: Approaches to Defining and Treating Mental Illness

This essay laid the theoretical groundwork for my research poster, introducing a framework that distinguishes ambiguous vs. unambiguous mental illness. It argues that unambiguous cases benefit most from biological approaches, while ambiguous ones require phenomenological and patient-centered care.

Cats: Journey to the First Word (Danish Preschool Practicum Case Study)

During a semester in Copenhagen, I observed the linguistic and social development of “Maria,” a Ukrainian refugee child navigating Danish, English, and Ukrainian. Her struggles and progress highlighted both the strengths and risks of Denmark’s socio-cultural model of early education. This practicum showed me how cultural context, inclusion, and pedagogy shape development. It greatly influenced my interest in culturally aware robots for caregiving.
