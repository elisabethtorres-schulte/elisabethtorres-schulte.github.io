About Me

I guide my work in human-centric tech innovation with one guiding principle: that technology should be developed to foster widespread human flourishing. I define this flourishing as people being healthier, safer and freer to lead dignified and autonomous lives. My focus is on using the latest tech innovations, from AI systems to robotics and bionics, to accelerate that flourishing.

My path was shaped by diverse research experiences, ranging from neural engineering and machine learning, to psychology, philosophy and art. I currently work in Gartner’s High Tech Innovation practice advising tech CEOs on AI strategy. Every role led me to my current goal of building technology solutions with tangible, widely beneficial impact.

I come to my work as an interdisciplinary thinker, trained in cognitive science and studying psychology, neuroscience, linguistics, philosophy, and computer science. My curiosity pushes me to the edges of what’s next: from HCI to human–AI interaction, human–robot cooperation, and eventually bionics. Through all of this, I maintain the conviction that we cannot afford to let emerging technologies replicate the worst of human systems, but have a moral imperative to build systems that empower people rather than excluding them from the benefits of technological progress.
